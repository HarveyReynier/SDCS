Methodology



Model specification

The predictive tool is based on a multiple linear regression that models the average pollution removal (per year in grams) for each London Plane tree based on carbon sequestration (per year in grams), spread (meters) and height (meters) . The model is described as follows:
y = b0 + b1x1 + b2x2 + b3x3
Where:
y = average pollution removal per tree
b0 is the constant term
b1 is the coefficient for the effect of carbon sequestration denoted x1
b2 is the coefficient for the effect of spread denoted x2
b3 is the coefficient for the effect of height denoted x3



Conclusion

This project was drafted by a group of students in order to communicate the presence and importance of the trees within Camden Council. Our means are twofolds: firstly, we visualized the location of "public" trees (trees that are within council-owned property) through a multi-layered map. Secondly, we interpreted council-provided data on the trees through some summaries and a regression analysis.

All information on this website is open to public access. The data was sourced from the Camden Council Tree Dataset.

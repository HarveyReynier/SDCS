About

This project was drafted by a group of students in order to communicate the presence and importance of the trees within Camden Council. Our means are twofolds: firstly, we visualized the location of "public" trees (trees that are within council-owned property) through a multi-layered map. Secondly, we interpreted council-provided data on the trees through some summaries and a regression analysis.
Our focal point was the impact of these amenity trees on the pollution removal across the Council as see in the map below. In further data analysis, we made a predictive tool that enables us to project the pollution removal of these trees over the coming half decade.

All information on this website is open to public access. The data was sourced from the Camden Council Tree Dataset.

Capital Amenity Value for Trees or Capital Asset Value for Amenity Trees (CAVAT) is a tool that enables authorities to quantify the value of amenity trees. Amenity trees are part of the urban forest system, which are trees within public domain and that authorities are therefore responsible for. CAVAT is a tool that is used by authorities in assets management to know the replacement value needed to compensate for a single tree or for a population of trees.
In the case of Camden Council, we focused on the CAVAT for the most common trees as they have the largest aggregated CAVAT values per ward. This might be related to how common they are in each ward, but also to the fact that they are the largest (in overall dimensions of height, spread and Diameter at Breast Height).
